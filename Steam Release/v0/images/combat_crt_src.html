Assault Resolution

Every Assault resolves in two steps. First the Pre-Attack volley — the defenders shoot the attackers crossing the ditch, hurting only the Ottoman. Then the Assault proper — the mutual clash on the strength-margin table, where both sides bleed.

1 · Pre-Attack — Defenders' Fire

Byzantine rolls d8 per committed garrison unit. Hits remove attacker steps before the clash, dropping the survivors onto a lower margin band.

Phase 1
Pre-Attack — Approach & Defenders' Fire

As the attackers cross the ditch under massed arrow, crossbow, gun and Greek-fire, the defenders shoot them up. This stage only ever hurts the attacker. The Byzantine rolls one d8 per garrison unit committed (1–4, the defender cap). Each die showing its top two faces (7–8) inflicts one attacker step loss, assigned by mandatory kill priority (§6.3.1). Casualties are removed before the margin is computed, so a good volley both kills attackers and weakens the assault.

Wall & Naval Defense are not dice — they stay flat modifiers to the Phase 2 margin. A fully-manned wall inflicts ~1 attacker casualty per assault; with a bashi absorber soaking the first hit, an elite dies only on a 2+ hit roll — occasional, not routine.

Firing positions degrade as the wall is battered — an intact wall shreds the approach, a breached one barely fires. Because the Ottoman only assaults Battered/Breached sectors, the fire they actually face is lighter, keeping elite loss rare.

Phase 2
Assault — Combat Results Table (Strength Margin)

The main clash. Column index is Margin = surviving Attacker Strength − Defender Strength (Wall/Naval Defense apply as a flat −6 / −3 / −1). Roll 1d10 and read Atk‑x / Def‑y: the Attacker loses x committed steps, the Defender loses y Garrison steps. A war of attrition — no one-roll breakthrough; concentrating more Attack simply grinds the garrison down faster.
