Production Notes

Sizing, print, and convention notes for the counter sheet.
Counter size
5/8″ (16mm) square — the GMT standard for line units (used across the COIN series and most GMT wargames). Large enough to hold an icon, name, and two stat boxes at arm's length; small enough to keep 100+ counters on two sheets. Shown here at 108px for legibility; export at 187×187px per counter (300dpi).
Leader tokens
Same 5/8″ footprint but circular, with a gold ring border — the shape swap alone reads "unique/named unit" on the table without needing a legend. Keeps them on the same die-cut sheet as the squares (most counter manufacturers cut mixed square/circle sheets without a surcharge).
Corners
Sharp, unrounded corners — classic GMT convention (rounding is more of a mainstream/Euro-game tell). A 1px hairline inset frame (.counter::after) does the "printed edge" job instead.
Stat boxes
Bottom-left = Attack (crimson), bottom-right = Defense (navy) — consistent across every unit regardless of faction color, so the eye always finds Atk/Def in the same two corners.
Reduced step (flip side)
Same base gradient as the full-strength face, with one lighter horizontal band crossing the middle (pale-gold for Genoese, a translucent white band elsewhere) — reads as "this unit" at a glance, but visibly weakened, without the busier repeating-stripe look. Stats drop, icon and name stay put. One physical counter carries both steps; losing a step is a flip, not a removal, until the reduced side is also eliminated.
Counter sheet layout
Standard sheets run 17×13 counters at 5/8″ (a ~176×176mm die-cut sheet, the common commercial size). At ~30–40 unique units × front/back, plan on one full sheet plus a partial second for leaders and markers.

Two-tone diagonal split (Genoese Company, Venetian Marines, Isidore's Legation) marks a unit as an allied foreign contingent rather than native Byzantine — purple stays constant as the Byzantine-side color, the second color is unique per contingent (Genoa's pale gold, Venice's teal, the Papacy's milky white), so each reads as "allied, and specifically which ally" at a glance.
